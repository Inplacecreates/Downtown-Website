# Photography sources: Downtown

The honest summary first.

**Stock photography will not work for Downtown.** The whole brand
rests on documentary, real-night, the-crowd-is-the-subject
imagery. Stock is the opposite: posed, generic, decontextualised,
and almost always tourist-coded when filtered for "Africa" or
"Kenya nightlife." Using stock would actively undermine the
section 6 photography rules in DESIGN.md.

The right answer is to **commission a photographer to shoot
Downtown on a real Saturday night, once.** That shoot becomes the
photo library for the next twelve to eighteen months. Repeat
annually or when the venue meaningfully changes.

The five sources below are ranked in order of how on-brand they
will land.

---

## 1. Direct commission from a Kenyan documentary photographer (recommended)

The closest fit for the brand. Hire one person, brief them on the
DESIGN.md section 6 photo style (raw, documentary, real-night,
sodium and bonfire light), give them a Saturday at Downtown, get
back a usable library.

Photographers to consider (verify portfolios are still live before
reaching out):

- **Osborne Macharia.** https://osbornemacharia.com/
  Already in the moodboard. Senior level, established rates, would
  set the photographic identity for the whole brand if his
  schedule allows.
- **Brian Otieno.** Search "Brian Otieno Kibera Stories" or look
  for his coverage of Nairobi street and night culture. Strong
  documentary instinct.
- **Sarah Waiswa.** Ugandan-Kenyan, based in Nairobi.
  Internationally exhibited, photographs East African creative
  scenes from the inside. Likely above budget for a single shoot
  but worth asking.
- **Joe Makeni** or other working Nanyuki-based event photographers.
  Often the most economical option, often the most authentic
  because they already know the venue. Ask Vin's network or check
  the Downtown Instagram comments for tagged photographers from
  past nights.

**What to brief:** the photographer should arrive at 8pm, stay
through to last orders, shoot on the floor not from a balcony,
favour 35mm or 50mm focal lengths, accept grain, accept motion
blur, avoid flash unless flash is part of the venue's existing
light. Hand them DESIGN.md section 6 verbatim.

**Indicative budget:** for a senior name (Macharia, Waiswa), think
KES 150,000 to 400,000 for the night plus edit. For a working
local photographer, KES 25,000 to 80,000. The cheaper option from
a photographer who already loves Downtown will usually beat the
more expensive option from someone who is shooting it as a job.

## 2. The Nest Collective (Nairobi)

**URL:** https://www.thisisthenest.com/
A Nairobi-based artist collective working across film,
photography, and writing. They commission and produce. If Vin wants
the shoot to be more than utility (think: a short film about a
Saturday night at Downtown that lives on the site's About page),
this is the call.

**Indicative budget:** project-based, expect mid-six-figure KES
for a film-and-stills package.

---

## 3. Mt Kenya University, Department of Communication and Media

**URL:** https://www.mksu.ac.ke/ (verify the department contact;
search "Mt Kenya University Department of Communication" or
"Photography" for current course leads)

Approach the photography or media programme lead about pairing
with a final-year student or a small group. Often produces
authentic, energetic work for very low fees plus credit. Carries
real risk (consistency, reliability, copyright clarity) but the
upside is exactly the local, real-night feel the brand needs, plus
ongoing access (the same students can return monthly).

**Indicative budget:** KES 5,000 to 20,000 per shoot, plus clear
written usage rights. Get the rights document in writing before
the shoot, not after.

---

## 4. Diverse-stock platforms (utility shots only, not hero)

For things the brand needs but does not need to commission
(category icons, blog illustrations if blog ever exists, generic
"map pin / location" imagery), these platforms reduce the risk of
stocky-tourist visuals:

- **Nappy.co** https://www.nappy.co/  Photographs of Black people
  in everyday contexts, free, often more natural than mainstream
  stock.
- **Tonl** https://tonl.co/  Diverse and inclusive stock, paid,
  higher quality than free sets.
- **Eyeem** https://www.eyeem.com/  Reasonable Africa coverage,
  curatable.

Use these only for utility imagery. **Never for hero or gallery.**

---

## 5. Adobe Stock or Getty, with Africa filters (last resort)

If Downtown needs imagery before the commissioned shoot can
happen (launch is gated and the shoot is two months out), Adobe
Stock and Getty have searchable East African content. Filter
aggressively for documentary over staged, real-light over studio,
and avoid anything tagged "lifestyle." Use sparingly and replace
with commissioned work as soon as it lands.

**URL:** https://stock.adobe.com/, https://www.gettyimages.com/

---

## Summary

The brand will feel correct only with commissioned photography.
Plan for one serious shoot in the first quarter after launch, and
either repeat annually or rotate through a small bench of local
photographers who shoot every two or three weekends. Source 4 and
5 are escape hatches, not strategies.
