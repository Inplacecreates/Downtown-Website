# Moodboard: Downtown

Eight curated references for the feeling Downtown should evoke,
spanning nightlife photography, venue interiors, type-as-graphic
posters, and material light. URLs are accurate as of 2026-05-14;
re-check before relying on them, the photo web rots faster than
the rest of the web.

Each entry: title, URL, what to look at, why it's here.

---

## 1. Wolfgang Tillmans, nightlife and club work

**URL:** https://tillmans.co.uk/works
**Look for:** the club series, the "Lights" body of work, anything
labelled rave or nachts.
**Why:** Tillmans is the canonical reference for nightlife as
intimate, communal, lit by whatever light is actually there.
Not glamorous. Not staged. Faces caught mid-conversation, mid-
dance, lit by a bar sign or a phone screen. This is the colour
temperature and emotional register Downtown's photography should
land in.

## 2. Khalik Allah, Harlem night portraits

**URL:** https://www.magnumphotos.com/photographer/khalik-allah/
**Look for:** the *Souls Against the Concrete* series and any of
his night street portraits.
**Why:** Sodium-streetlight portraits of people in their element.
This is exactly the lighting Downtown's bonfire and string-light
nights will read like. Direct reference for warmth from a
non-glamorous light source.

## 3. Osborne Macharia, Kenyan documentary and culture work

**URL:** https://osbornemacharia.com/
**Look for:** his Magadi series, Nyanza series, anything where he
photographs the Kenyan creative scene from the inside.
**Why:** Macharia photographs Kenyans the way Kenyans want to be
seen. Confident, styled, present. Anti-tourist gaze. The hero
photography for Downtown should sit in this lineage, not in the
National Geographic one.

## 4. The Lot Radio, the homepage as a programme strip

**URL:** https://www.thelotradio.com/
**Look for:** the live broadcast bar at the top, the way the
schedule reads as the brand, the documentary photos behind it.
**Why:** Already cited in DESIGN.md section 9 as a key reference.
Visit it specifically with the marquee strip in mind, this is what
ours should land near.

## 5. Fonts In Use, condensed display typography on signage

**URL:** https://fonts.inuse.com/typefaces/in-use/big-shoulders
**Look for:** real-world uses of Big Shoulders Display and related
sign-painter condensed faces.
**Why:** Our display font, used in the wild. Gives a feel for the
range of personality Big Shoulders can hit, from industrial poster
to civic signage to event identity. Useful for calibrating where
Downtown sits on that range (closer to the event identity end).

## 6. Boiler Room session photography

**URL:** https://boilerroom.tv/
**Look for:** the still photography accompanying any session, not
the videos. Crowds packed in, DJ booth lit only by the booth
light, faces in flash, no glamour.
**Why:** The crowd-as-subject documentary register. Boiler Room's
visual language is the closest reference for "how to photograph a
night so that the viewer wants to be there next Saturday" without
being a tourist photo.

## 7. Public Records Brooklyn, venue space photography

**URL:** https://publicrecords.nyc/about
**Look for:** their interior photographs, especially the bar and
listening room shots in low light.
**Why:** A community venue photographed at its actual operating
light level, not lit for a brochure. The closest analogue we have
to how Downtown's makuti-and-string-light environment should be
captured for the site's utility shots (directions, private hire).

## 8. NTS Radio, type-led programming graphics

**URL:** https://www.nts.live/shows
**Look for:** the way show cards are laid out, the running schedule
strip, the use of one accent colour against black and white.
**Why:** Already cited in DESIGN.md section 9. Pin this one to
your tab while making programme card decisions. The discipline of
"the schedule is the design" is exactly what we want.

## What is deliberately not on this board

- Drone shots of any venue, anywhere.
- Glamour-lit cocktail photography or "luxury hospitality" sets.
- Festival aerial crowd shots.
- Heavily styled "lifestyle" agency photography.
- Anything that reads as Berlin-warehouse-coded (smoke, laser,
  silhouette-only). Downtown is warm; that aesthetic is cold.

If a reference creeps into this board that fits any of those, it
got there by mistake and should be removed rather than kept "for
contrast."
